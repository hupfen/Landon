Clients



Titan Coding

July 2013 — March 2014

Titan Coding provides API integration services, which ranges all the way from translating JSON feeds to building full functionality into pre-designed sites, for companies in industries ranging from direct marketing to telecoms. In July, Titan had more projects than staff, and deadlines where fast approaching. Enter Landon—by the end of the summer, three C#-backed sites were delivered on schedule. From there, Landon led the development team to produce substantial functional and UX improvements for the company's existing clients, and helped produce the company's first iOS application.


LocalEdge

June 2011 — May 2013

LocalEdge provides online solutions for small and medium businesses. These include online advertising through Google and Bing, as well as reputation management, SEO, and other related services. Landon has been used to update an acquired online ad management and reporting system, written in C# ASP.Net with a mix of front-end toolkits used in various capacities. In particular, Landon helped replace outdated third-party components, expand the report catalog, and improve the efficiency of routine tasks.


BrandLogic

June 2009 — December 2010

BrandLogic devises and implements rebranding efforts for a wide array of companies and non-profits. Their designers and writers rebrand companies from the ground up, while developers fine-tune their proprietary CMS system to help keep the company's site on message. Landon helped the small development team build and test new site designs and features, including prototype concepts, and helped create internal tools to streamline and accelerate the new-site development process—turning a week-long process into an hour's work.


TLF Graphics

June 2008 — May 2009

TLF Graphics is a small, B2B print company that produces a wide array of printed products in-house, thanks to their staff of skilled print experts and a constantly-updated array of print devices. Landon helped investigate, plan, and implement TLF's fresh e-commerce efforts. The result was a PHP-based site using customized, open-source e-commerce tools which could handle many times more custom printing orders than the rest of the sales team.
